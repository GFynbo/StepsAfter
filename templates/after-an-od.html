After an Overdose

Emergency: Call 911 and then text (424) 297-5085 for immediate instructions on dealing with an overdose.

Dealing with an overdose can be hard and traumatizing, but we're here to help.

The first step to dealing with an overdose is to understand that it is not your fault and the fact that you feel responsible is common.

Opiate addictions are a growing issue in the United States. Many people are susceptible to getting addicted when using the drug. In 2014 over 14,000 died. Opioid overdoses occur not from just small regular prescription, but from using multiple substances or taking too much of what should be their prescribed dose.

Opiates are commonly referred as Vicodin, OxyContin, or morphine. It can cause death and even brain damage. Many people get “normalized” by the drug, unable to function with the regular use of it. Vicodin also has tendencies for an acetaminophen overdose, which can lead to liver failure and or a damaged liver.

If you are with someone who has an opioid overdose, one should lie him on his side to prevent the person from choking on their own spit. Try to help the person remain consciousness by conversing. If one has an overdose, he or she should be very CLOSELY monitored so that they do not get hurt.

If you or a loved one is under the risk of an opioid overdose, it may be handy to carry around Naloxone, which helps essentially stop an overdose for a bit of time. When administered you should call 911.
